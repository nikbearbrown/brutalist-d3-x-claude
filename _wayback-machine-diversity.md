**Race / ethnicity (visible representation):** 14 white, 2 Black (Du Bois, Wells), 1 Japanese (Homma). **Flag:** heavily white. The non-Western and non-white representation is thinner than the gender balance. See below.

**Geographic / national origin:**

## Flags to Address

1. **Race skew (high priority).** Three non-white figures across seventeen. To improve, candidate swaps include:
   - **Gladys West** for any computing/programming chapter — mathematician whose work was foundational to GPS, profoundly undertold.
   - **Annie Easley** for the Claude Code / programming chapter — Black American mathematician at NASA, rocket-fuel and energy work.
   - **Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar** could replace Priestley for time series if the connection to time-domain astronomy holds — though the timeline-as-medium fit is weaker.
   - **Mei Wending** could replace Priestley — 17th-century Chinese mathematician/astronomer, visual representation of celestial cycles.

2. **Non-Western thinness.** Only Homma. To strengthen:
   - **Mei Wending** as above.
   - **Aryabhata** for early mathematical visualization (too ancient; weak fit for "should be better known").
   - **Hsue-shen Tsien (Qian Xuesen)** for systems engineering — could fit Chapter 17 alongside or instead of Gilbreth.

3. **The "Western Black American" axis is concentrated.** Both Du Bois and Wells are in adjacent chapters (01 and 05). Spreading them or substituting one might help — but both fits are substantive and the figures are different enough (Du Bois as visual social scientist, Wells as data journalist) that the doubling reads as intentional rather than redundant.

## Constraints That Resist Easy Fixes

The spec said: *"The link to the chapter's concepts should be substantive. A figure who worked* on *the thing, not just* near *it."* Several non-Western figures who would diversify the roster sit at "near," not "on" — Mei Wending was a mathematician working on astronomical computation, not specifically on visualization-as-encoding. Forcing such a figure into a chapter on, say, candlestick charts would weaken the lesson. The current set prioritizes substantive fit over category-balance; the trade-off is named here so the next pass can revisit deliberately.

The **gender balance is robustly defensible** — every woman on this list did the work the chapter teaches, and the gender breakdown corrects a real historiographic imbalance without compromising fit.
